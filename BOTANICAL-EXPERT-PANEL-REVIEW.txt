DR. LINNEA VOSS — BOTANICAL ILLUSTRATION & VISUAL ACCURACY
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

FINDINGS:

STRENGTHS:
✓ SVG iconography is botanically *plausible* without being literalist — excellent design choice
✓ Visual progression from seed→sprouting→budding→bloomed follows a recognizable morphological sequence
✓ Seed icon (solid circle with dashed ellipse) correctly represents cotyledon dormancy — accurate
✓ Sprouting icon (vertical stem + root circle + leaf emergence) shows correct root-shoot axis development
✓ Budding icon (central stem + bilateral leaf/bud structures + 6 total organs) mimics dicot structure well
✓ Bloomed icon (5-petaled flower with radiating ellipses + central disk) is aesthetically sound

CONCERNS:
⚠ Sprouting emoji shows 🌿 (leafy branch), but the stage is labeled "sprouting" — minor: sprouting should
  still show the cotyledon + emerging primary leaf, not a mature leaf stage. Visual emoji is 1–2 weeks ahead.

⚠ Budding visual is generic (6 leaves/structures radiating). In reality, buds show:
  - Opposite or alternate leaf arrangement (dicots vs monocots vary)
  - Bud scales protecting developing flowers
  - Much tighter clustering than the icon shows
  The icon is MORE accurate for a "leafy stage" than a true "budding" stage.

⚠ Bloomed icon (5 petals) assumes angiosperm dicot structure. Many plant families have different petal counts
  (3=monocots, 4=Cruciferae, many=Rosaceae). Using 5 petals is a design simplification, not botanical truth.
  This is acceptable for metaphor; however, the choice may feel arbitrary to botanists.

BOTANICAL ACCURACY SCORE: 7/10
- Morphologically sound progression for a generic dicot
- Oversimplifies structural diversity within flowering plants
- Acceptable for metaphor; not a literal field guide

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
PROF. KAI TANAKA — GROWTH SYSTEMS & BIOMIMICRY
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

FINDINGS:

THE CORE ISSUE — THE 4-STAGE LIFECYCLE OVERSIMPLIFIES NATURE:

Real plant life cycles follow patterns like:
1. Germination (seed→radicle emergence): REPRESENTED as "Seed"
2. Vegetative growth (leaf/stem development): REPRESENTED as "Sprouting"
3. Reproductive maturation (flower bud formation): REPRESENTED as "Budding"
4. Anthesis/flowering (flower opens, pollination): REPRESENTED as "Bloomed"
5. Senescence/seed set: NOT REPRESENTED — CRITICAL GAP
6. Dormancy/overwintering: NOT REPRESENTED — CRITICAL GAP

SPECIFIC GROWTH BIOLOGY ISSUES:

❌ BLOOMING ≠ COMPLETION
  Botanical fact: Flowering is NOT the end of the plant cycle. After bloom comes:
  - Fruit development (2–8 weeks in many species)
  - Seed maturation (weeks to months)
  - Dispersal or dormancy

  The app treats "Bloomed" as 100% complete (progress = 85–100%).
  In nature, a bloomed flower is barely 60–70% through its annual cycle.

  VERDICT: Metaphorically intuitive (people understand "in bloom" as flourishing),
  but scientifically dishonest. A strategic initiative that is "bloomed" might still need
  seed dispersal (market adoption, scaling).

⚠ MISSING INTERMEDIATE STATES:
  The 4-stage model forces all plans into linear progression. But real growth has:
  - Plateau phases (dormancy, feedback loops)
  - Regression (frost damage, blight) — app has "at-risk," but no visual state for "wilting/decline"
  - Cyclical patterns (perennials resprout, deciduous plants drop leaves)

  The app needs explicit "WILTING" or "DORMANT" states for plans that stall, fail, or need restart.

✓ STRENGTHS:
  - Seed→Sprouting→Budding→Bloomed IS directional and generally progressive
  - The quarterly progression (Q1–Q4) implicitly mirrors temperate growing season (spring→summer→fall)
  - Color gradient (teal→sage→gold) mimics chlorophyll→carotenoid shift (green→yellow) seen in autumn

BIOMIMICRY ASSESSMENT:

The loading animation ("Growing your insights...") cycles through all 4 stages with a pulsing
seed icon. This mimics rapid time-lapse growth, which IS authentic to how plants grow.
However, it skips the senescence and dormancy phases entirely. A true biomimetic model would
include a 5th or 6th stage.

GROWTH SYSTEMS SCORE: 5.5/10
- Directional progression is sound
- Blooming-as-completion is metaphorically satisfying but scientifically inaccurate
- Missing critical lifecycle stages (senescence, dormancy, failure states)
- Quarterly alignment to temperate seasons is clever but underdocumented

DR. ROSA MENDEZ — PHENOLOGY & SEASONAL RHYTHMS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

FINDINGS:

TEMPORAL MAPPING ANALYSIS:

The app uses Q1–Q4 (calendar quarters) as target date containers:
- Q1 (Jan–Mar): Northern hemisphere EARLY SPRING
- Q2 (Apr–Jun): LATE SPRING / EARLY SUMMER
- Q3 (Jul–Sep): MID-SUMMER / EARLY FALL
- Q4 (Oct–Dec): FALL / EARLY WINTER

Does this align with a real botanical cycle?

✓ INSIGHT: Yes, for temperate annual plants:
  - Seed germination typically occurs in Q1–Q2 (spring)
  - Vegetative growth peaks Q2–Q3 (summer)
  - Flowering occurs Q2–Q4 (varies by species)
  - Senescence/dormancy Q4–Q1 (fall/winter)

  The quarterly structure implicitly mirrors this. HOWEVER, it only works for the Northern
  Hemisphere. Southern Hemisphere users would experience the seasons inverted.

⚠ PHENOLOGICAL DISCONNECT:

The app does NOT adjust the lifecycle stages based on start date. For example:
  - A plan starting in Q3 (July) should not reach "Bloom" until Q4 or Q1 (faster cycle)
  - A plan starting in Q4 (October) hits dormant season — growth stalls
  - A plan starting in Q1 (January, cold) germinates slowly — "Sprouting" may take 8+ weeks

The app treats all plans as if they are on identical growth schedules, regardless of start date.
This is phenologically unrealistic.

✓ STRENGTH: The metadata shows startDate and targetDate fields are captured in the data model.
            The visual system could be enhanced to reflect real phenological timing.

⚠ CRITICAL ISSUE: "At-Risk" flag exists for stalled plans, but there's no visual or temporal
  reason *why* a plan would stall. In nature, stalling = dormancy (winter) or stress (drought,
  pest). The app should display expected dormancy windows if a plan aligns with a seasonal
  low-growth period.

COLOR PALETTE AS PHENOLOGICAL SIGNAL:

Colors used:
- Seed/Early: Teal (#2A6B6B) — cool, dormant
- Sprouting: Sage green (#6B7F70) — active growth, chlorophyll
- Budding: Sage (lighter, #8A9B8E) — pre-reproductive, high chlorophyll
- Bloomed: Gold (#D4A574) — carotenoid-rich, senescent or ripe

✓ SCIENTIFICALLY SOUND: This progression mimics actual leaf/fruit color changes in temperate plants:
  - Green (chlorophyll) during growth
  - Yellow/gold (carotenoid) during ripening or senescence

✗ METAPHOR GAP: The gold color is associated with "complete," but in nature, gold often signals
  *decline* or *end-of-cycle*. An alternative reading: "Bloomed Gold" = autumn harvest, which IS
  complete, but carries connotations of decline, not flourishing.

PHENOLOGICAL ACCURACY SCORE: 6/10
- Quarterly structure loosely aligns with Northern Hemisphere growing seasons
- Color palette reflects real pigment shifts in plants
- Missing: phenological awareness (dormancy windows, stress states)
- Does not account for actual elapsed time or seasonal context
- Southern Hemisphere phenology is invisible

SOREN LINDQVIST — LANDSCAPE ARCHITECTURE & SPATIAL COMPOSITION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

FINDINGS:

GARDEN METAPHOR COHERENCE:

The app is called "Strategic Garden," with core screens named:
1. Garden View (radial clusters on canvas)
2. Bloom View (single objective expands)
3. Present Mode (full-screen narrative)

Does the metaphor hold spatially?

✓ GARDEN LOGIC:
  - A garden has multiple plantings (objectives)
  - Each planting has component plants (tactical plans)
  - Visual separation between objectives mimics crop blocks or garden beds
  - The canvas is "living" — particles drift, suggesting environmental presence
  - Color-coding by status (teal→sage→gold) mimics seasonal progression across a garden

✓ RADIAL CLUSTERING: Each objective card shows tactical plans as "petals radiating from a center."
  This is a brilliant spatial metaphor — it mimics:
  - Compound flowers (sunflower, daisy)
  - Radial garden designs (Victorian sunburst beds)
  - Organic growth patterns (no grid, natural asymmetry)

✗ CRITICAL SPATIAL ISSUE: The actual layout description in the code shows:
  ```
  "Objective card shows the health and status of your tactical plans."
  ```
  But there's no evidence in the HTML of ACTUAL radial/petal rendering. The objectives appear
  to be displayed as a grid of cards with standard shapes, not true botanical radial layouts.

  The visual metaphor is described in the design brief but NOT implemented in the current code.
  The SVG icons are radial, but the objective cards themselves are RECTANGULAR grid elements.

  VERDICT: The garden metaphor is partially **aspirational** rather than fully realized in the UI.

✗ LANDSCAPE COMPOSITION WEAKNESS:
  A real garden has:
  - Focal points (specimen plants)
  - Pathways (visual flow)
  - Scale variation (tall in back, short in front)
  - Seasonal succession (early bloomers, then late bloomers)
  - Negative space (air, breathing room)

  The app's garden is abstract enough that traditional landscape hierarchy is lost.
  The grid layout undermines the organic metaphor.

✓ PRESENT MODE (FULL-SCREEN): This is where the spatial metaphor shines. A full-screen
  presentation mode with "swipe/arrow navigation" mirrors moving through a garden space.
  This is architecturally sound.

SPATIAL COMPOSITION SCORE: 6.5/10
- Radial/petal metaphor is conceptually strong
- Rectangular grid layout undermines the organic garden feel
- Lack of true spatial hierarchy, focal points, pathways
- Full-screen Present Mode redeems the metaphor through immersive experience
- Particle animation adds environmental presence (positive)

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
DR. AMARA OKAFOR — ETHNOBOTANY & CULTURAL SYMBOLISM
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

FINDINGS:

PLANT SYMBOLISM ACROSS CULTURES:

The app uses the following plant/botanical symbols:
1. Seed (🌱) — germination, new life, potential
2. Sprout (🌿) — growth, hope, renewal
3. Leaf/bud — maturity, development
4. Flower (🌸, 5-petaled bloom) — beauty, completion, flourishing
5. Teal color — growth, vitality
6. Gold color — abundance, completion, ripeness
7. Sage green — calm, healing, wisdom

CULTURAL CONTEXT ANALYSIS:

✓ UNIVERSALLY POSITIVE SYMBOLS:
  - Seeds → new beginnings (nearly universal positive)
  - Flowers → beauty and growth (positive across cultures)
  - Gold → prosperity and completion (positive in many traditions)

  No major risk of offense in these base symbols.

✓ COLOR PALETTE SENSITIVITY:
  - Sage green: Associated with healing in European and Indigenous traditions. Positive.
  - Teal: Modern color without heavy historical symbolism. Neutral-to-positive.
  - Gold: Universally associated with wealth and completion. No major cautions.

⚠ MINOR CONSIDERATIONS:

1. THE CHERRY BLOSSOM EMOJI (🌸):
   - Used for "Bloomed" status
   - Cherry blossoms have specific cultural meaning in East Asia (Japan: ephemeral beauty, transience)
   - In Japanese culture, cherry blossoms bloom briefly and fall — metaphorically closer to *decline*
     than to *completion*
   - Could confuse Japanese users who see "Bloomed" as signifying impermanence, not final triumph

   RECOMMENDATION: Consider using a generic flower emoji or no emoji at all. If keeping 🌸, ensure
   documentation explains Western cultural reading (completion), not Japanese (transience).

2. WILTING/AT-RISK SYMBOL:
   - The app uses a triangle warning symbol (⚠) for at-risk plans
   - This is universally understood as "caution" — no cultural offense
   - However, there's no visual plant metaphor for "wilting" or "diseased"
   - The color for at-risk is Warm Amber (#C07A6B), which can signal warmth/vitality in some cultures
   - Consider: Is "at-risk" visually distinct enough from the normal progression?

3. GARDEN METAPHOR IN COLONIZED REGIONS:
   - The "Strategic Garden" metaphor is rooted in Victorian/European garden design
   - In regions with strong Indigenous land practices (agroforestry, polyculture), the formal
     garden concept might feel colonial or extractive
   - HOWEVER: The abstract implementation (not depicting literal gardens) mitigates this risk

   VERDICT: Safe, but worth acknowledging in product documentation that the metaphor is
   design-inspired, not literal land management.

4. LOTUS CONCERNS:
   - The app does NOT use lotus imagery
   - Lotus has deep spiritual/cultural significance in Buddhist and Hindu traditions
   - No risk here; but if future releases add "sacred bloom" states, lotus must be handled carefully

✓ DIVERSITY OF PLANT SYMBOLISM:
  The app treats flowering plants generically (no species preference). This is GOOD — it avoids
  privileging any one cultural botanical tradition.

CULTURAL SENSITIVITY SCORE: 8/10
- Plant symbolism is universally positive
- Cherry blossom emoji carries unintended transience connotation (minor)
- Color palette has no major cautions
- Garden metaphor is abstract enough to avoid cultural appropriation concerns
- Recommendation: Document the Western cultural reading of symbols; consider emoji alternatives

ROUND 2: CROSS-PANEL DEBATE & CONSENSUS
================================================================================

MODERATOR: Thank you all for independent assessments. Let's identify critical tensions.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
KEY DEBATE 1: "IS BLOOMING REALLY THE END?"
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Dr. Voss: The SVG is beautiful, but we're all uncomfortable with the same issue:
in nature, blooming is not the end. It's the reproductive phase.

Prof. Tanaka: Exactly. A plant in bloom must still:
- Be pollinated
- Develop fruit/seeds
- Mature those seeds
- Either disperse them or enter dormancy

The app conflates "flower opens" with "project complete." They're not the same.

Soren: From a design perspective, I understand WHY the team chose this. "In bloom" =
beautiful, aspirational, flourishing. It's satisfying to see something "bloom." But it's
botanically false.

Dr. Rosa: And the problem compounds with the golden color. Gold = autumn, senescence,
end of cycle in nature. It works symbolically for "done," but it undercuts the vitality
the design intends to convey.

Dr. Okafor: In Japanese culture, it's worse — cherry blossoms bloom and immediately
fall. Users from that tradition might read "Bloomed/Complete" as "this is about to fail."

CONSENSUS RECOMMENDATION:
The app SHOULD introduce a 5th stage: "SEEDED" or "PROPAGATED"
- Represents seed maturation, market adoption, scaling beyond first-market success
- This would be post-blooming
- Color: Deep gold or bronze (harvest/maturity without the golden-hour glow)

OR reframe the entire metaphor:
- Bloomed = "Ready for market" (mid-cycle)
- Add "Harvested" or "Scaled" as true completion (post-cycle)

This would require minimal UI changes but massive metaphorical gain.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
KEY DEBATE 2: WHAT ABOUT FAILURE STATES?
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Prof. Tanaka: The app has an "atRisk" boolean flag, but no visual "Wilting" state.
In nature, plants fail — frost kills them, disease strikes, drought stuns growth.

Dr. Voss: Right. The current system has 4 growth stages + at-risk overlay. But there's
no stage for a plan that has DEFINITIVELY FAILED or been DISCONTINUED.

Soren: This is actually a critical user need. A plan might be:
- Indefinitely paused (dormant for winter, then resumes)
- Permanently abandoned (project sunset, no resurrection)
- Regressing (was in Blooming, now back to Budding due to new market challenge)

The current design assumes monotonic forward progress. That's not realistic.

Dr. Rosa: And if we're using botanical metaphor seriously, we need dormancy states.
A plant in winter is not "at risk" — it's dormant. There's a difference. One resolves
with time; the other requires intervention.

CONSENSUS RECOMMENDATION:
Add two visual states to the lifecycle:

1. DORMANT (pale gray or white with frost texture)
   - Plans that are intentionally paused
   - Will resume at a known date
   - Color: #D3D0CA (muted gray, frost-like)
   - Icon: Frozen seed or leafless branch

2. WILTED (red-brown or burgundy)
   - Plans that have failed or been discontinued
   - Cannot return to growth
   - Color: #8B5A5A (muted red-brown, like dried/dead plant matter)
   - Icon: Drooping flower or bare branch

This gives users realistic lifecycle language.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
KEY DEBATE 3: THE LOADING ANIMATION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Dr. Voss: The loading screen cycles through all 4 growth icons with a pulsing seed.
It says "Growing your insights..."

Prof. Tanaka: That's brilliant. It's showing real-time growth. But it accelerates
the entire cycle to maybe 2 seconds total. Is that honest?

Dr. Rosa: In nature, that cycle takes weeks or months. The animation compresses it
for UI purposes. But does it feel *right*?

Soren: As a designer, I think it works. It's clearly metaphorical — nobody believes
we're growing actual plants. It signals "loading" in a branded way.

Dr. Voss: I agree. The animation is authentic to how plants grow, just massively
time-compressed. And it respects prefers-reduced-motion settings, which is good.

CONSENSUS: No change needed. The animation is metaphorically sound and respectfully executed.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
KEY DEBATE 4: THE RADIAL PETAL LAYOUT
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Soren: I need to flag this: the design brief describes objectives as "radial clusters"
with "petals radiating from center." But the actual implementation shows rectangular
grid cards.

Dr. Voss: So the metaphor is promised but not delivered?

Soren: Exactly. The SVG icons ARE radial (5-petaled flowers). But the objective containers
are standard React grid elements. There's a disconnect.

Dr. Rosa: Does the code show actual radial rendering anywhere?

[Panel reviews grid configuration in HTML]

Prof. Tanaka: No. It's `gridTemplateColumns: 'repeat(auto-fill, minmax(320px, 1fr))'`
That's a standard CSS grid. Not radial at all.

Dr. Okafor: So the garden metaphor is partially aspirational?

Soren: Yes. And I think that's actually okay — the grid is clean and functional.
But if we're calling this a "garden" and the icon is radial, there should be
visual consistency.

CONSENSUS RECOMMENDATION:
Either:
A) Embrace the grid and rename view "Status Dashboard" instead of "Strategic Garden"
   - Less metaphorical but more honest

B) Implement actual radial layouts for objective clusters using SVG or CSS radial shapes
   - True to metaphor but requires significant refactoring
   - Worth considering for v5.0

For v4.0, recommend documenting that "Garden" is metaphorical inspiration, not literal layout.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
KEY DEBATE 5: SOUTHERN HEMISPHERE USERS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Dr. Rosa: The quarterly structure (Q1 = Spring, Q4 = Winter) is Northern Hemisphere.
In Australia, South Africa, Argentina — the seasons are opposite.

Prof. Tanaka: For a global product, this is a real problem. A plan starting in
Q1 might be starting in early *fall* for Southern Hemisphere users.

Dr. Voss: Does the app capture user timezone or location?

Soren: The code doesn't show that capability. Target dates are stored as ISO dates.
The metaphor assumes Northern Hemisphere phenology.

CONSENSUS RECOMMENDATION:
Add optional "region" or "hemisphere" selector to adjust phenological context.
- Display "North" or "South" hemisphere phenology labels on quarter designations
- Example: "Q3 (Mid-Summer in N. Hemisphere, Mid-Winter in S. Hemisphere)"
- This is low-effort, high-impact for global users

Current implementation is Northern-Hemisphere-centric. Not a deal-breaker,
but limits international usability of the metaphor.

OVERALL ASSESSMENT: 6.5/10 BOTANICALLY COHERENT

The ImpactQ v4.0 botanical metaphor is CONCEPTUALLY STRONG but MECHANICALLY INCOMPLETE.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
WHAT WORKS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

1. ✓ The 4-stage progression (Seed→Sprouting→Budding→Bloomed) is directionally sound and
     mimics a simplified annual plant lifecycle

2. ✓ SVG icons are botanically plausible and morphologically progressive without being
     literalist (good design choice)

3. ✓ Color palette progression (teal→sage→gold) mirrors real pigment shifts in nature
     (green photosynthesis → yellow carotenoids in senescence)

4. ✓ Loading animation is metaphorically authentic and respects accessibility preferences

5. ✓ Plant symbolism is culturally positive with only minor emoji-specific cautions

6. ✓ Quarterly structure loosely aligns with Northern Hemisphere growing seasons

7. ✓ Particle animation and breathing effects add environmental presence (good biomimicry)

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
WHAT DOESN'T WORK / NEEDS FIXING
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

1. ✗ BLOOMING ≠ COMPLETION (Botanical inaccuracy)
   The app treats "Bloomed" as the endpoint, but in nature, blooming is mid-cycle.
   Flowers must develop fruit, mature seeds, and either disperse or dormancy.

   FIX: Introduce a 5th stage: "SEEDED" or "PROPAGATED" post-blooming, OR reframe
   Bloomed as "market-ready" and add "Harvested" as true completion.

   PRIORITY: HIGH (undermines the entire metaphor's scientific integrity)

2. ✗ MISSING FAILURE & DORMANCY STATES
   The app has "atRisk" but no visual "Wilting" or "Dormant" stages.
   Plans can't regress, pause indefinitely, or fail — only move forward or stall with a warning.

   FIX: Add two new visual states:
   - DORMANT (gray, pale color, explicit "will resume" label)
   - WILTED (burgundy/red-brown, "discontinued" label)

   PRIORITY: HIGH (improves UX realism and botanical honesty)

3. ✗ RADIAL METAPHOR NOT FULLY REALIZED
   Design brief promises "petals radiating from center," but actual layout is rectangular grid.
   The metaphor is aspirational, not implemented.

   FIX (Choose one):
   A) Accept grid layout, rename view from "Strategic Garden" to "Strategic Dashboard"
   B) Implement true radial SVG layouts (more ambitious, better metaphor fidelity)

   PRIORITY: MEDIUM (design cohesion, not core functionality)

4. ✗ NORTHERN HEMISPHERE PHENOLOGY BIAS
   Quarterly structure (Q1=Spring) assumes Northern Hemisphere. Southern Hemisphere users
   see inverse seasons.

   FIX: Add optional region selector or hemisphere toggle; update quarter labels with
   dual phenological context ("Q3: July–September, Mid-Summer [N], Mid-Winter [S]")

   PRIORITY: MEDIUM (affects global users, easy to implement)

5. ⚠ CHERRY BLOSSOM EMOJI TRANSIENCE CONNOTATION
   🌸 is beautiful but carries "ephemeral/decline" meaning in East Asian cultures.

   FIX: Replace with generic flower emoji (🌼, 🌷) or remove emoji, keep visual icon.

   PRIORITY: LOW (minor cultural nuance, not offensive)

6. ⚠ COLOR AMBIGUITY: GOLD = COMPLETION OR DECLINE?
   Gold (#D4A574) works for "warm/ripe" but also signals autumn senescence (decline).

   FIX: Option 1 — update gold to a brighter yellow (#F4D4A5) to signal vitality, not decline
         Option 2 — pair gold with explicit "flourishing" language in UI

   PRIORITY: LOW (mostly aesthetic; current implementation works but could be clearer)

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
RANKED PRIORITY FIXES (For v4.0 vs. v5.0)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

MUST FIX BEFORE LAUNCH (v4.0):
1. Introduce DORMANT + WILTED states (2–3 day refactor)
2. Reframe or extend "Bloomed" stage to reflect that completion isn't bloom (1–2 day refactor)
3. Add Northern/Southern hemisphere phenology toggle (1 day, huge global usability gain)

SHOULD FIX BEFORE v4.0 (Lower effort, high quality):
4. Replace cherry blossom emoji with genus-neutral flower
5. Clarify "Strategic Garden" metaphor or implement true radial layout in design roadmap

CAN DEFER TO v5.0 (Ambitious, excellent-to-have):
6. Implement true radial SVG layouts for objective clusters
7. Add phenological dormancy window predictions (if plan aligns with seasonal low-growth period)
8. Adjust color palette if gold ambiguity tests poorly with users

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
PANEL CONSENSUS STATEMENT
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

ImpactQ v4.0 uses botanical metaphor AUTHENTICALLY and RESPECTFULLY. The core 4-stage
lifecycle is scientifically grounded, visually elegant, and culturally inclusive.

HOWEVER, the metaphor is *incomplete*. By treating blooming as the end-goal rather than
a mid-cycle phase, the design creates a subtle lie about how growth actually works. Strategic
initiatives don't end when they bloom — they scale, distribute, integrate into operations.

The addition of 5th stage (SEEDED/PROPAGATED) and explicit failure states (DORMANT, WILTED)
would transform this from a "nice botanical-inspired UI" into a metaphor that **teaches**
users something true about biology AND strategy: growth is cyclical, not linear.

We recommend these additions before launch. They require minimal UI refactoring and maximum
metaphorical coherence.
